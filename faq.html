Common Questions About Abbotsford Hospital

What services does Abbotsford Hospital provide?

Abbotsford Hospital offers emergency care, surgical procedures, inpatient hospitalization, diagnostic imaging, laboratory testing, and specialized medical services across multiple departments.

As an acute care hospital, Abbotsford Hospital coordinates patient care services through a network of specialized departments. Emergency care is available around the clock for urgent and critical conditions. Surgical procedures range from routine operations to complex interventions, supported by pre-admission processes and post-operative care. Inpatient hospitalization provides comprehensive monitoring and treatment for patients requiring extended stays, while outpatient clinics offer scheduled assessments and follow-up care.

Access to many specialized medical services requires specialist referrals from a primary care provider. Diagnostic imaging services include X-ray, ultrasound, CT, and MRI, while laboratory testing encompasses blood work, microbiology, and pathology. The hospital works within the broader British Columbia healthcare system to ensure continuity of care and appropriate resource allocation.

How do I access the emergency department at Abbotsford Hospital?

The emergency department is open 24/7 for urgent and critical medical conditions. Walk-ins are accepted, or call 911 for life-threatening emergencies.

When you arrive at the Abbotsford Hospital emergency department, you will be assessed through a triage process. Triage nurses evaluate the severity of your condition and assign a priority level based on clinical urgency. Patients with life-threatening conditions such as chest pain, severe bleeding, difficulty breathing, or stroke symptoms are seen immediately. Those with less urgent concerns may experience longer wait times, as the emergency department prioritizes cases by medical need rather than arrival order.

Wait times vary throughout the day and depend on patient volume and the complexity of cases being treated. Bring identification, a current medication list, and information about allergies and existing medical conditions. If your situation is not urgent, consider contacting HealthLink BC at 811 for guidance on whether emergency care is necessary or if an alternative care pathway would be more appropriate.

What are the visiting hours at Abbotsford Hospital?

Visiting hours vary by department. Most units allow visitors during daytime and early evening hours; check with your specific ward for exact times.

Each unit within Abbotsford Hospital sets visiting hours based on patient care needs, infection prevention protocols, and the nature of treatments provided. General medical and surgical wards typically welcome visitors during daytime and early evening periods, while intensive care and specialized units may have more restricted schedules to protect patient rest and privacy.

Before visiting, contact the specific ward or unit to confirm current visiting policies. Infection prevention measures may limit the number of visitors or require health screening at entry points. Respect rest periods, especially during early morning and late evening hours when patients require uninterrupted sleep for recovery. Visitors should follow hospital guidelines regarding noise levels, mobile device use, and hand hygiene to support a safe and healing environment for all patients.

How can I schedule an outpatient clinic appointment?

Contact the hospital with a valid referral from your primary care provider or specialist. Outpatient clinic appointments are typically arranged through a referral-based system rather than direct patient booking.

To schedule an outpatient clinic appointment at Abbotsford Hospital, follow these practical steps:

  • Obtain a referral: Your family physician or another healthcare provider must submit a referral to the appropriate specialty clinic. The referral should include your medical history, reason for consultation, and relevant test results.
  • Gather information: Have your personal health number, contact details, current medication list, and any recent diagnostic reports ready when the clinic contacts you.
  • Confirm appointment details: Once the referral is processed, the clinic will contact you to schedule an appointment. Confirm the date, time, location within the hospital, and any preparation requirements such as fasting or medication adjustments.
  • Prepare questions: Write down questions or concerns you want to discuss during your appointment to make the most of your consultation time.
  • Arrange transportation: Plan your travel to the hospital and consider whether you will need someone to accompany you, especially if sedation or procedures are involved.

Wait times for outpatient appointments vary by specialty and clinical urgency. If your condition changes or worsens while waiting, contact your referring physician to update the referral priority.

Before You Arrive: Documents, Accessibility, and Support

Proper preparation helps ensure a smooth experience at Abbotsford Hospital. Whether you are visiting the emergency department, attending an outpatient clinic, or supporting a hospitalized family member, having the right documents and information ready streamlines registration and care delivery.

Essential items to bring to Abbotsford Hospital
Item Why It Helps Example
Government-issued identification Confirms identity and links to health records Driver's license, passport, or BC Services Card
Personal Health Number (PHN) Enables billing and record access within BC health system Number on your BC Services Card
Current medication list Prevents drug interactions and ensures continuity of treatment Prescription bottles or written list with dosages
Allergy information Alerts care team to avoid harmful substances Drug allergies, food allergies, latex sensitivity
Referral or requisition forms Authorizes specific tests, imaging, or specialist consultations Paper or electronic referral from your physician
Previous test results Provides clinical context and avoids duplicate testing Recent blood work, imaging reports, or specialist letters
Emergency contact details Enables communication with family if needed Name and phone number of next of kin

Abbotsford Hospital is committed to accessibility and patient-centered care. If you require language interpretation, mobility assistance, or accommodations for sensory or cognitive needs, inform registration staff or your care team upon arrival. Support persons are welcome in most areas of the hospital, and staff can provide guidance on parking, wayfinding, and amenities.
